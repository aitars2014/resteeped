The problem with tea memory

You tried a tea last month that was incredible. Smooth, honey-sweet, lingered perfectly. You'd order it again in a heartbeat. But you can't remember the name. Was it from Harney? Or that other shop? Was it an oolong or a black?

This happens constantly. You drink dozens of teas a year. Without notes, the good ones blur together and the details fade.

What Resteeped tracks for you

  • Tea name, brand, and type from a database of 8,000+ teas. No typing names from scratch.
  • Your rating (1-5 stars). Quick and honest.
  • Tasting notes. What did it actually taste like? "Honey and toast" is better than "good."
  • Brew parameters. Water temp, steep time, leaf amount. So you can recreate that perfect cup.
  • Your collection. Everything you've tried, organized and searchable.

Why digital beats paper

Some people keep a physical tea notebook. There's charm in that. But a phone app has real advantages:

  • Searchable. "What was that oolong I loved in October?" takes 2 seconds.
  • Sortable. Show me everything I rated 4+ stars. Show me all the green teas. Show me what I bought from this shop.
  • Always with you. You'll have it at the tea shop, at a friend's house, at a restaurant.
  • Pre-populated. Resteeped already has 8,000+ teas in the database. Search, tap, log. No writing out full tea names by hand.
